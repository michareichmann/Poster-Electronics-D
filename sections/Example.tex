\posterbox{Example - Full Adder}{
	An adder is a digital circuit that performs addition of numbers. A full adder adds binary numbers and accounts for values carried in as well as out. A one-bit full-adder adds three one-bit numbers, often written as $A$, $B$, and $C_{\z{in}}$; $A$ and $B$ are the operands, and $C_{\z{in}}$ is a bit carried in from the previous less-significant stage. The circuit produces a two-bit output: Output carry and sum typically represented by the signals $C_{\z{out}}$ and $S$, where the sum equals $2C_{\z{out}} + S$.\\
